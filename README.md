<h2>Phases</h2>

<h3>Phase 1: Infrastructure & Compliance (May – July 2026)</h3> 
This phase focuses on building the "skeleton" of the engine during the summer break. The 
primary goal is to establish a server-first environment by setting up a Linux VPS in the Mumbai region to ensure low-latency connectivity and meet SEBI’s static IP mandates. Key technical tasks include automating the Dhan OAuth 2.0 and TOTP handshake to allow for daily, headless authentication. Additionally, we will build a robust WebSocket Ingestion Layer to stream and log live tick data into a PostgreSQL database. Success here means the machine "wakes up" and logs data perfectly without human help. 
<h3>Phase 2: The Logic & Backtesting Engine (August – November 2026)</h3>
Once the plumbing is ready, we build the "brain." This cycle is about mathematical validation. You will develop the Volatility Picker to filter the Nifty 200 and build an Event-Driven Backtester. Unlike simple simulators, this engine will account for slippage and latency to provide a realistic view of performance. We will use Pandas and NumPy for vectorized research to calculate the Expected Value of your strategies. The outcome is a verified strategy that shows a statistical edge over years of historical data, proving the "Why" behind every trade before risking capital. 
<h3>Phase 3: Risk Guardrails & Paper Trading (December 2026 – January 2027)</h3> 
In this phase, we install the "brakes." The focus shifts to Pre-Trade Risk (PTR) management. You will build a Finite State Machine to track order statuses and a Kill Switch that triggers if daily loss limits are hit. Using Redis for sub-millisecond risk checks, the system will enter the Paper Trading phase—executing virtual trades through the live API. This allows us to stress-test the code against real-market quirks like network timeouts and requotes. By the end, the system must run for 30 days with zero logic crashes or safety breaches. 
<h3>Phase 4: Institutional Execution & AI Scaling (February – May 2027)</h3>
The final stage is about optimization and deployment. We will integrate an AI Sentiment Layer using NLP to filter trades based on global news and market trends. To reach elite performance levels, we will port the core execution logic from Python to Rust (via PyO3), reducing latency to a sub-millisecond range. Finally, we will deploy real capital with minimum lot sizes to verify the engine's performance in the live market. This phase completes the technical showcase, proving your ability to manage a high-concurrency, AI-driven financial ecosystem while maintaining your academic excellence.

<h2>Proper Explanation of Each Phase</h2>

<h3>Phase 1: Infrastructure & Compliance (The Foundation)</h3>
Phase 1 is about moving from a "student with a laptop" to a "systems architect with a server." During your summer break (May 8th – July 4th), your primary goal is to build a professional-grade environment that runs independently of your personal computer. In the world of high concurrency trading, your local machine is a liability—it has high latency, unstable internet, and a dynamic IP that violates the SEBI 2026 algorithmic trading mandates. 
The first step is provisioning a Linux VPS (Virtual Private Server), specifically in the Mumbai region (like AWS ap-south-1). This ensures you are physically close to the exchange servers, 
reducing "network hop" delays. You will need to master Bash and SSH to manage this server. You aren't just running a script; you are managing a 24/7 environment. This includes setting up a firewall (UFW) and ensuring your trading engine restarts automatically if the server reboots, using tools like Systemd. 
The second pillar is Automated Compliance. You cannot manually log in every morning at 9:00 AM. You will write a Python-based OAuth 2.0 Bridge. This script will use your secret API keys and the pyotp library to generate a Time-based One-Time Password (TOTP), perform the handshake with the Dhan API, and retrieve a fresh Access Token. This token is the "key" that allows your machine to communicate with the market. 
Finally, you will build the Data Pipeline. You will use Asynchronous WebSockets (asyncio) to open a persistent connection to the market. This "Ticker Tape" will stream thousands of price updates (ticks) every second. You will learn to parse this high-speed JSON data and store it in a PostgreSQL database. This is critical: you cannot build the "Brain" in Phase 2 if you don't have a clean, local history of how the market actually moved during the summer. By July 4th, your system should be a silent, automated machine that wakes up, logs in, and records every heartbeat of the market without you touching a single key. 
 
<h3>Phase 2: The Logic & Backtesting Engine (The Brain)</h3>
Phase 2 is the transition from "Plumbing" to "Alpha Generation." Occurring from August to 
November, this is the most mathematically rigorous stage of ‘Project _’. The goal is to prove that your strategy has a statistical edge before a single rupee is ever risked. Most amateur traders skip this and rely on "feel"; you will rely on Vectorized Research and Event-Driven Simulation. If you cannot prove the "Why" behind your strategy’s performance during this phase, you are faking productivity and setting yourself up for failure. The first component is the Multi-Stage Scanner. You will build a logic gate that filters the Nifty 200 down to a "Watchlist" of five stocks. This isn't about guessing; it's about identifying liquidity and volatility. You will use Average True Range (ATR) to find stocks that are moving and Volume Profiling to ensure you aren't trading in "ghost markets" where you can't exit a position. Once you have your stocks, you move into Vectorized Research using Pandas and NumPy. Here, you apply indicators (like Mean Reversion or Momentum) across years of historical data. 
You are looking for a positive Expected Value (E[X]). If E[X] isn't positive, your strategy is a hobby, not a business. However, vectorized math is a "lie" 
because it assumes perfect execution. To fix this, you will build an Event-Driven Backtester. This is a sophisticated Object-Oriented (OOP) system that simulates the market tick-by-tick. It accounts for Slippage (the price moving before your order hits) and Latency. By the end of November, you must conduct a Deep Introspection Audit. You will use Python’s cProfile to identify bottlenecks in your code. If the "Global Interpreter Lock" (GIL) is slowing down your simulation, this is when we decide if the 
core engine needs to be ported to Rust. Success here is not a "high win rate"; it is a robust, non overfitted backtest that survives a 9.0+ CGPA academic schedule. 
 
<h3>Phase 3: Risk Guardrails & Paper Trading (The Brakes)</h3> 
Phase 3, occurring between December 2026 and January 2027, is the "Safety First" stage. This is the bridge between the "Lab" (Phase 2) and the "Arena" (Phase 4). The primary objective is to build the Pre-Trade Risk (PTR) Engine. In high-frequency environments, a single logic error can result in an "infinite loop" of orders that could wipe out your capital in seconds. The PTR acts as a digital firewall, inspecting every order packet for size, margin, and frequency before it ever leaves your VPS. 
To achieve this, you will implement a Finite State Machine (FSM). This is a critical engineering concept for your technical showcase. An FSM ensures your system knows exactly where an order is at all times—whether it is Pending, Partially Filled, or Rejected. Without an FSM, your system might "hallucinate" its position size, leading to catastrophic risk errors. For submillisecond lookups, you will utilize Redis, an in-memory data store. Redis allows your Risk Engine to check your daily P&L and margin limits in microseconds, ensuring the "Safety Layer" doesn't become a "Latency Bottleneck." During this phase, you will also build the Paper Trading Bridge. This connects your engine to the live Dhan API using virtual funds. This is where you encounter "Real World" friction: API timeouts, sequence gaps in WebSockets, and exchange rejections. You are no longer trading against a "clean" CSV file; you are trading against the messy,unpredictable reality of the Indian market. Finally, you will develop the Emergency Kill Switch. This is a manual and automated override. If the system loses more than a set percentage (e.g., 2%) in a day, or if you press a button on your private Django-based Command Center, the system must instantly cancel all pending orders and flatten all positions. This phase coincides with your Theory FAT Exams, meaning your "Academic Lockdown" protocol must be strictly enforced. You are only allowed to monitor the 
paper trades—no new code is to be written during the 48–72 hour pre-exam window. 
Success in Phase 3 is defined by a system that is "Bored"—running for weeks without a single logic crash or safety violation.

Phase 4, spanning February to May 2027, is the "Endgame" where ‘Project _’ transitions from a sophisticated prototype into an institutional-grade technical showcase. This phase is designed to prove that you can handle the complexity of modern financial engineering, coinciding with the GSoC 2027 application window and your Semester 4 finals. Here, we move beyond pure technical analysis and integrate Alternative Data and Low-Latency Architecture. The first major evolution is the AI Sentiment Layer. Technical indicators (like those built in Phase 2) only tell you how the price is moving; they don't tell you why. You will develop a pipeline using NLP Frameworks (HuggingFace/Transformers) to scrape and analyze financial news headlines and social media trends in real-time. By assigning a "Sentiment Score" to your filtered stocks, the AI acts as a final gatekeeper, pausing trades if it detects a "Market Shock" or negative news spike that your technical indicators would otherwise miss. 
The second evolution is the Performance Pivot. To reach elite Quant standards, you must solve the "Python Bottleneck." You will rewrite the core execution and WebSocket handling logic in Rust, utilizing PyO3 to bind it back to your Python research layer. This "Hybrid Architecture" is a high-tier engineering flex: it allows you to keep the flexibility of Python for strategy development while achieving the sub-millisecond latency of C++ through Rust's memory-safe, zero-cost abstractions. This is exactly what top-tier HFT (High-Frequency Trading) firms look for in candidates. 
Finally, you will move to Live Capital Deployment. This is the ultimate test of your 9.0+ CGPA mindset. You aren't trading for "luck"; you are trading to verify that your engineering is profitable in the real world after taxes and brokerage.
